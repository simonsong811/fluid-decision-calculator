Post-30 mL/kg Reassessment Visualizer

A research-use visualization for septic shock patients who have reached approximately 30 mL/kg crystalloid. The page is organized around the study's central question: after the initial threshold, how should residual perfusion need and fluid-intolerance risk be reassessed before another bolus?
Research visualization only. Not intended to prescribe fluid dose or replace bedside reassessment.
Main message for investigators
After 30 mL/kg, the useful question is not "more fluid or not"; it is whether persistent need and fluid tolerance point to a monitored reassessment.
1. Why 30 mL/kg?

It creates a concrete post-guideline checkpoint where the next clinical action is uncertain and trial-ready.

2. What changed after audit?

The appealing fluid-first signal was sensitive to endpoint coupling and time-window definition.

3. What remains useful?

Four bedside phenotypes separate prognosis, tolerance risk and candidate response signals.

Clinical use boundary

Use this display to structure a post-threshold conversation: Is there residual perfusion need? Is there fluid-intolerance risk? If a fluid challenge is still considered, the data support small-volume reassessment with close monitoring rather than automatic large-volume escalation.

Before any additional bolus, reassess PLR/VTI or stroke-volume response when available, capillary refill, MAP trajectory, oxygenation, lung ultrasound, venous congestion, and urine output.
